The Experience

Dive into the vibrant heart of music at the Vancouver Island MusicFest brought to you by the Comox Valley Folk Society and a collective of 1,300 amazing volunteers. This unforgettable weekend of camraderie, festival magic, food and drink transforms the Courtenay Exhibition Grounds into a musical wonderland, boasting 5 to 6 dynamic stages. Embrace diversity in every chord and inclusive community spirit in every note, all in a positive, caring and family-friendly setting with a sprawling campground.

Immerse yourself in an eclectic mix of Roots, Folk, and Global music, featuring a stellar lineup of artists from local gems to global icons.

This festival isn't just about music—it's a cultural odyssey. With an array of workshops, jam-packed jam sessions, and soul-stirring concerts, it's a living tribute to the rich tapestry of Western Canadian Folk Festivals.

Nestled in a picturesque setting, our campgrounds lie along the serene Tsolum River, surrounded by lush forests. Whether you're bringing an RV, camper van, or a tent, our rustic camping experience is designed for those who share our love of nature, music and community spirit. Explore the full experience

Comox Valley Folk Society

At our core, we celebrate diversity in our volunteers and performers, and are dedicated to an eco-friendly, sustainable vibe. Join the movement where positivity, music, culture, and environmental consciousness converge!

We are a charitable entity rocking since 1995! Our mission: to orchestrate an electrifying roots and world music festival every summer in the Comox Valley. Inspired by the Canadian Folk Music Festival blueprint, we collaborate with Canada's top festivals like Winnipeg, Calgary, Regina, Edmonton, Vancouver, and more, syncing talent and innovative fest strategies.

Indigenous Territorial Acknowledgement

The Vancouver Island Music Festival proudly honors the vibrant heritage of the Indigenous peoples whose ancestral lands serve as the backdrop for our celebration of music, friendship, and community. We pay homage to the Puntledge, E’iksan, and K’omoks peoples, whose enduring connection to this land resonates in our festival's spirit today. Join us in a heartfelt tribute to the rich history and ongoing legacy that shapes our gathering.
